About Chelel Memorial School

Our Unique Environment

Chelel Memorial School is totally unique in terms of the beauty and space available. The 5 acres is made up of school buildings, boarding houses & gardens, state-of-the-art sports and arts facilities, sports pitches, a riding school and paddocks, forest areas, nature trails, and a low ropes course.

Wildlife is encouraged at the school, and we have the privilege of hosting a variety of native species within our grounds, including birds, small mammals, and butterflies that add to the natural beauty of the environment. The school takes pride in its commitment to sustainability and environmental education, with students actively participating in conservation projects and wildlife monitoring. Our nature trails and forest areas serve as outdoor classrooms where students can learn about ecology, biodiversity, and the importance of preserving natural habitats. The serene and picturesque surroundings provide an inspiring setting for both academic and extracurricular activities, fostering a deep connection to nature and a sense of responsibility towards the environment.

As a Round Square School, we are committed to protecting and enhancing the beautiful environment around us. We have a number of student-led environmental groups who work with the Estate Team to manage and address environmental issues. In the past, they have focused on recycling, education on biodiversity, sustainable sources of energy, and indigenous forests.

Our Purpose

The educational philosophy of Chelel Memorial School is to focus on creating independent, lifelong learners. We have high academic expectations of the pupils and expect them to put as much effort into their co-curricular activities.

Academic excellence, engaging co-curricular programs, commitment to pastoral care, emphasis on leadership, and, above all, Christian living lead to our students’ great academic achievement, personal development, and independence.

History

Chelel Memorial School was founded by Mr. Vincent in 2020. With an initial enrolment of 15 children, 'Chelel' has grown into one of the leading international schools in the region, catering to over 200 students.

Mr. Vincent used his skills to successfully set up and run the school. He feels his work is in direct obedience to God’s calling to build a Christian school, hence the strong emphasis on Christian love and care in the running of the school to date. In 2021, the Senior School was added to provide a caring, safe, and friendly boarding environment for students aged 14 to 18.

Developing Leaders

Chelel Memorial School believes that in order for our students to thrive in whatever sphere of life they choose, they need not only qualifications but also the faith, character, and personal skills to make a difference.

Developing leaders starts from a young age. At the Prep School, the Leader in Me program is incorporated into the everyday life of pupils, with an emphasis on service. They regularly visit schools and charities to assist those less fortunate than they are.

At the Senior School, leadership skills are honed through involvement in the Round Square program, which emphasizes key values and provides practical opportunities.

At the Sixth Form College, students go through a structured two-year leadership program (Chelel Memorial School Leadership Award) which teaches professional leadership skills and culminates in a university-accredited qualification. It is designed to build and mold the next generation of leaders who have integrity and a vision for Africa.

Educational Philosophy

The educational philosophy of Chelel Memorial School focuses on creating independent learners. We have high academic expectations of the pupils who are given opportunities to lead and achieve in different areas of learning.

We endeavor to send out well-rounded individuals. Students are expected to put as much effort into their co-curricular activities as they do academics. Academic excellence, engaging co-curricular programs, commitment to pastoral care, emphasis on leadership, and, above all, Christian living lead to our pupils’ great academic achievement, personal development, and independence.

The Community

Having operated in the area since 2020, we recognize our Corporate Social Responsibility and interdependence with the local community. As a result, we work closely with the local police, local government, and community leaders to ensure we have a positive impact on our local area.

Key community service projects include:

  • Guaranteeing a regular supply of clean water to the local village from our water sources.
  • Running a nursery school for our workforce children.
  • Fundraising to support local charities.
  • Sending students into the community to support key projects.

Security and Visitors

Protected by an electric fence which surrounds the site, a well-trained team of security staff and dogs manage the main gate and patrol the site at night. This security is in place to ensure the safety of students, staff, and visiting parents.

We would appreciate your support in complying with our security measures. These include security checks at the gate and registration of your staff who might visit the site. Please notify us 72 hours in advance of their arrival by emailing info@chelelmemorialschool.co.ke. (We reserve the right to refuse entry without prior authorization).

Students are also required to provide written approval to exit the School, for their own protection. For safety reasons, within the School grounds, there is a strict speed limit of 15 km/hr and clearly labeled designated parking areas.

Parents are invited to the School on six weekends each year, called VISO weekends. These are important and very enjoyable times for students and parents alike. Please see the Term dates and VISOs page for more information and future dates.
